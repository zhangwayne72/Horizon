## [European Gas Prices Near Iraq War Highs](https://www.ftchinese.com/story/001110401) ⭐️ 2.0/10

European natural gas benchmark prices have surged nearly 50% in the past month, approaching levels not seen since the early days of the Iraq War, driven by a heatwave and concerns about meeting winter storage targets. This price surge threatens energy affordability for European households and industries, and highlights the region&\#x27;s vulnerability to geopolitical disruptions, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz, through which 19% of global LNG trade passes. The market is especially sensitive to any potential closure of the Strait of Hormuz, as Qatar and the UAE, major LNG exporters, rely on it for 93% and 96% of their exports respectively. EU countries aim to fill gas storage to 90% capacity by November 1, but current fill rates lag behind the 5-year norm.

rss · FT中文网-今日焦点 · Jul 22, 16:00

**Background**: Natural gas prices in Europe have been volatile since the energy crisis began in 2021, exacerbated by the Russia-Ukraine conflict. The EU has set mandatory storage targets to ensure supply security, but extreme weather and geopolitical tensions can disrupt filling progress. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global LNG trade, and any disruption there can quickly impact European prices.
